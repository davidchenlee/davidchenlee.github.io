PAST INTERESTS

During my Ph.D. studies, I investigated emergent phenomena that arise in complex systems of many interacting quantum particles. To this end, I used advanced electro and magneto-optical techniques, such as optical lattices and stimulated Raman scattering, to trap, cool, and manipulate ultracold gases with extremely high precision

For my B.S. dissertation, I used scanning tunneling microscopy to measure, analyze, and characterize the surface of thin conductive films. This work was part of a study to investigate the effects of electron-surface scattering on the transport properties in metals.
