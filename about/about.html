PAST INTERESTS

During my Ph.D. studies, I used ultracold matter to investigate emergent states in strongly correlated systems [2-6]. In particular, I used advanced electro and magneto-optical techniques–such as optical lattices and stimulated Raman scattering–to trap, cool, and manipulate atomic gases with extremely high precision

For my B.S. dissertation, I studied the effects of electronic surface scattering in metals. Specifically, I used scanning tunneling microscopy to measure, analyze, and characterize the surface of thin conductive films [1].
