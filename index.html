David Chen

Postdoctoral researcher at the Myers Lab at the Max Planck Institute for Cell Biology and Genetics (MPI-CBG) and the Center for Systems Biology Dresden(CSBD)

I am building a high throughput two-photon microscope to image whole fixed tissue as large as 1 cm3, in-situ, and with sub-micron resolution. This novel technology will be used to explore the spatial organization of cells across scales. As part of this project, I have established a new Meso-imaging Laboratory at the MPI-CBG.

This project is carried out in close collaboration with the Zerial Lab. We aim to digitally reconstruct an entire mouse liver to understand how cells form meso and macroscopic strucures. For more information, check out their seminal work: "A versatile pipeline for the multi-scale digital reconstruction and quantitative analysis of 3D tissue architecture"
